Mason Lynass

Mason Lynass is a software engineer, music creator, and A/V producer, currently based in Seattle, WA.

Mason is currently working as a freelance software engineer and web developer, building websites for clients and learning new skills & languages by developing personal projects. He completed a 15-week Software Engineering course at the Flatiron School, developing and deploying Full-Stack web applications and exploring JavaScript, React.js, Ruby on Rails, SQL & PostgreSQL, Sinatra & ActiveRecord, HTML, CSS, and Git.

Mason has performed original generative electronic music, given recitals of contemporary percussion repertoire, played drums in various pop & rock bands, and performed at festivals and concert halls across the country. He has worked as a sound designer, engineer, and music producer with film production studios, independent artists, podcast productions, and other freelance clients.

Mason won the Portland Percussion Group's Call for Scores with his work Tesserae, composed for percussion trio or quartet. Since the Portland Percussion Group premiered Tesserae in May of 2017, the piece has been published through Tapspace and performed by ensembles around the United States.

Mason earned a Master of Music degree at the University of Washington and a Bachelor of Music degree at the University of North Texas, studying percussion performance with renowned faculty including Bonnie Whiting, Christopher Deane, Ed Soph, and Paul Rennick.

Mason Lynass

Mason Lynass is a software engineer, music creator, and A/V producer, currently based in Seattle, WA.

Mason completed a 15-week Software Engineering course at the Flatiron School, developing and deploying Full-Stack web applications and exploring JavaScript, React.js, Ruby on Rails, SQL & PostgreSQL, Sinatra & ActiveRecord, HTML, CSS, and Git. He is currently learning new languages & frameworks, building projects with collaborators, and transitioning to a career in Software Engineering.

Mason graduated from the University of Washington with a Master of Music degree in percussion performance. At UW, Mason studied with Dr. Bonnie Whiting, played in the Partch Ensemble and various UW ensembles, and recorded and released a recital of solo percussion repertoire in June of 2020.

Mason has performed as a soloist and as an ensemble member in venues and concert halls across the country. As a soloist, Mason’s work was most recently featured at his Masters recital, at the Racer Sessions, and Seattle’s John Cage Musicircus. Outside of his solo work, Mason has played festivals and toured the west coast with Señor Fin and Baby Jessica, performed at the Percussive Arts Society International Convention with the UNT Percussion Ensemble and the Santa Clara Vanguard, and hung out at SoSI, Chosen Vale, and Inuksuit NYC.

Mason won the Portland Percussion Group’s Call for Scores with his work Tesserae, composed for percussion trio or quartet. The Portland Percussion Group premiered Tesserae in May of 2017, and the piece was published through Tapspace in 2019.

Mason earned a Bachelor of Music degree in percussion performance from the University of North Texas, where he studied with Christopher Deane, Paul Rennick, and Ed Soph.
